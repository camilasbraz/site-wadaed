About

Originally from Montevideo, Uruguay, I completed my undergraduate studies in Electrical Engineering at UDELAR in 1991. I further advanced my academic journey by earning my M.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering from the Universidade Federal de Santa Catarina (UFSC) in Florianópolis, Santa Catarina, Brazil, in 1995 and 2002, respectively.

In 2004, I commenced my academic career at DEE-UFMG as an adjunct professor. My research took a significant turn in 2019 when I served as a postdoctoral researcher at the Institut für Controlling und Unternehmensrechnung at the Technische Universität Braunschweig (ICU-TUB) in Germany. During this period, my work primarily focused on the regulation of electricity transmission companies and the development of models for measuring operational cost efficiency.

Methodological and Empirical Contributions to the Transmission Operations Service Cost Regulation 2018 - 2021

Since the late 1980s, reforms in the electric power sector have emerged in various countries. These reforms aim to stimulate firms to increase efficiency, maximizing community well-being. Regulation regimes in the electricity sector have been introduced using benchmarking methodologies. In Brazil, ANEEL uses the Data Envelopment Analysis (DEA) approach for calculating operational costs in the electricity sector. The challenge is to develop customized benchmarking models reflecting the sector's specificities. The innovation of this proposal by NESP/UFMG (Brazil) and ICU/TUB (Germany) is to provide an advanced benchmarking approach including criteria that reflect companies' performance and the development of case-specific DEA models. This new DEA approach will differ from ANEEL's current approach and could serve as a template for energy regulation in other countries.
